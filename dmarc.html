Data Analytics & Visualization

DMARC Food Pantry

Client DMARC Network
Role Data Analyst
Year 2024
Deliverables Predictive Model, Dashboard
01

Background

The Des Moines Area Religious Council (DMARC) Food Pantry Network operates the largest food pantry network in Iowa. They collect substantial data on the individuals they serve, but raw numbers alone often fail to tell the complete story of community need. To better allocate resources and understand food insecurity, DMARC sought deeper insights into their service data relative to broader demographic trends.

02

The Challenge

While internal records showed who was visiting the pantry, the data existed in a silo. It was difficult to identify which specific demographic groups were disproportionately at risk or underserved compared to the general population. Additionally, anticipating future demand—such as seasonal spikes or economic shifts—was reactive rather than proactive, making inventory management difficult.

03

The Solution

I led an initiative to integrate DMARC's private client data with public American Community Survey (ACS) data. This enriched dataset allowed us to map internal usage against external poverty and demographic statistics. I designed comprehensive data visualizations to communicate these trends clearly to non-technical stakeholders. Furthermore, I developed a predictive model using historical data to forecast future client needs and pantry traffic.

04

The Result

The project transformed raw data into actionable intelligence. The visualizations successfully identified specific at-risk populations that required targeted outreach. The predictive model provided DMARC with accurate forecasts for future demand, enabling more efficient staffing and inventory planning. This data-driven approach has empowered the organization to serve the Des Moines community more effectively.
