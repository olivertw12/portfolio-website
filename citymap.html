City of Shorewood Map

Project Type Community Prototype
Role Illustrator
Year 2024
Deliverables Rug Design, Map
01

Background

This project began as a university initiative to design a functional prototype that would benefit the local community of Shorewood, Minnesota. The goal was to create an educational tool that fostered a sense of place and belonging for younger residents, helping them understand the layout of their city and its relationship to surrounding townships in an engaging, tactile way.

02

The Challenge

Learning city layouts and navigation can be abstract and difficult for children when relying on traditional 2D maps or digital screens. The challenge was to translate complex geographic data—including roads, landmarks, and city borders—into a format that was intuitive, playful, and physically interactive, without sacrificing geographic accuracy.

03

The Solution

I developed a custom illustrated rug that functions as a large-scale play mat. The design features a stylized yet accurate map of Shorewood and its neighboring areas. Key landmarks like city hall, parks, and schools were illustrated with distinct icons to serve as navigational anchors. The roads were designed to be wide enough for toy cars, encouraging active play that reinforces spatial learning.

04

The Result

The final prototype effectively depicts Shorewood, Minnesota, along with its real city landmarks and surrounding townships. It successfully bridges the gap between a toy and an educational resource, providing a tangible way for children to explore their community from the safety of a classroom or living room.
