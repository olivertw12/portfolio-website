Shorewood Internship

Client City of Shorewood
Role Graphic Design Intern
Year 2024
Deliverables Fillable Forms, PDFs
01

Background

The City of Shorewood, Minnesota, manages a wide variety of administrative processes that require documentation, from permits to internal HR forms. As a local government entity, it is crucial that these documents are professional, accessible, and easy for both citizens and staff to process. I joined the team as a Graphic Design Intern to assist in modernizing their communication materials.

02

The Challenge

Many of the city's existing documents were outdated, visually inconsistent, and difficult to use. Some were static files that required printing and hand-filling, creating friction for users and inefficiencies for data entry staff. The challenge was to audit these legacy documents and transform them into a cohesive system that aligned with the official Shorewood Branding Kit while drastically improving usability.

03

The Solution

I utilized Adobe Acrobat and InDesign to redesign and engineer a suite of fillable PDFs. I strictly applied the Shorewood Branding Kit—using approved typography, color palettes, and logos—to ensure every document looked official and trustworthy. Beyond aesthetics, I focused on user experience by adding interactive form fields, dropdown menus, and clear instructional hierarchy to make digital completion intuitive.

04

The Result

The project resulted in a library of modernized, user-friendly documents that streamlined administrative workflows. The shift to fillable PDFs reduced paper waste and data entry errors, providing a smoother experience for Shorewood residents. The visual consistency across all forms reinforced the professionalism and reliability of the city government's brand.
